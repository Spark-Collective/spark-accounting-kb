# Sponsoring costs

Sponsoring (money or means given to an event/project/organisation in return for visibility or benefits) is deductible, but the rate depends on **what the company gets back**.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **In return for publicity: 100% deductible** as a professional cost , e.g. the company's logo/name on posters, flyers, banners, social media, a website link. The spend is clearly aimed at visibility. [astro]
- **In return for benefits: 50% deductible** as **representation costs** , e.g. receiving event tickets used to invite staff or clients. The representation element caps it at 50%. [astro]

> Confirm the current treatment with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be); document the publicity received (photos of the banner, the web mention) to support the 100% case.

## Booking

Split by nature: the publicity portion (100%) and any representation portion (50%) book to their class-6 accounts (see [[chart-of-accounts-mar]]); the non-deductible fraction of the representation part is a tax add-back ([[year-end]], planned). The 50% representation cap mirrors the logic on [[restaurant-and-meals]].
