# Fines paid by the company

A company can pay a traffic fine (e.g. on a [[car-and-mobility|company car]]) from its account, but the fine is **not deductible** , it is a **disallowed expense** (verworpen uitgave).

## The rule

- **Fines are 100% non-deductible.** The company still pays corporate tax (20% or 25%, see [[corporate-tax-basics]]) on the fine amount as a disallowed expense. [astro]
- **Total cash out = fine + ~20%** of the fine in tax. Still cheaper than paying it privately from already-taxed income. [astro]

## Booking

Book the payment to a class-6 account, then add it back in full in the corporate-tax computation as a disallowed expense ([[expense-categorization]] flags it). The booking is normal; the deduction is denied.
