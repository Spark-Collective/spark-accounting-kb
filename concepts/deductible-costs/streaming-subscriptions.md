# Streaming subscriptions (Spotify, Apple Music, ...)

A music/streaming subscription is deductible **only if the service is genuinely part of the work** , you must be able to show it is needed to carry out the professional activity, not a private comfort.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **Deductible** when the streaming service is intrinsic to the activity , e.g. a **podcaster** who publishes on Spotify, or an **artist** distributing their music. [astro]
- **Not deductible** when the link is weak , e.g. an IT consultant or lawyer who "concentrates better" with music. The tax authority treats this as a **private cost** and will reject it at a control. [astro]
- **Watch the terms of use:** consumer streaming plans are for personal, non-commercial use , playing them publicly in a shop/salon/office is not allowed and does not make them deductible. [astro]

> The deductible/not test is fact-specific; confirm with the accountant or [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be) for a borderline case.

## Why it matters

This is a clean example of the **professional-character test** that governs many small costs: the burden is on the taxpayer to show the business need. The same logic gates [[restaurant-and-meals|business meals]] and other dual-use items like [[reading-glasses]].
