# Reading glasses

Reading glasses (leesbril) can be booked as a professional cost, but only **partly** and **not with certainty** , they are a classic dual-use item where the line between professional and private is blurred.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **Up to ~50% deductible** as a professional cost. [astro]
- **No guarantee at a control:** the tax authority may take a stricter view of what counts as a genuine professional cost; the deduction can be challenged. [astro]
- **Blue-light-filter glasses** are often pitched for screen work but are also seen as a **personal aid** usable outside work, which weakens the deduction. [astro]

> The 50% is a practical ceiling, not a statutory certainty , confirm with the accountant, and keep documentation of the professional need.

## Why it matters

Another instance of the **professional-character test** ([[streaming-subscriptions]]): for items usable both at work and privately, expect scrutiny and deduct conservatively. Contrast with a clearly professional tool like [[ict-equipment]] (100% income-tax deductible).
