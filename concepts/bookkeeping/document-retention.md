# Document retention period

A law of 20 November 2022 **extended the general retention period from 7 to 10 years** for accounting documents and invoices , for both VAT and direct taxes.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **General: 10 years**, counted from **1 January of the year following** the financial year (or the invoice date). Applies from **income year 2022 (assessment year 2023)** onward. [search]
- **Older years stay at 7 years:** documents for income year 2021 and earlier keep the old 7-year term. [search]
- **Real-estate documents** with VAT deducted (purchase deeds, renovation contracts, the related invoices): **15 years**, sometimes **25 years**, because the VAT revision period runs that long. [search]
- **Investment invoices:** keep until **7 or 10 years after the year of the last depreciation**. [search]
- **Paper or digital** are equivalent, provided **authenticity, integrity, and legibility** are guaranteed for the whole period. [search]

> Confirm the current terms with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be) , the extension is recent and the start year matters.

## Why it matters

Drives the agent's archival policy: a one-person BV in 2026 keeps everything from FY2022 until end-2032, and real-estate VAT files far longer. Ties to the [[chart-of-accounts-mar|books]] and the [[annual-accounts-nbb|filed annual accounts]].
