# When is VAT deductible (the conditions)

Input VAT on a purchase is only recoverable if the basic conditions are met. Getting this right is the heart of [[booking-a-purchase-invoice|booking a purchase invoice]].

## The conditions

1. **Professional purchase.** Only VAT on purchases for the business is deductible. A 100% private purchase gives no deduction; a **mixed-use** item (phone, laptop, [[car-and-mobility|car]], [[ict-equipment|tablet]]) is only **partly** deductible. [astro]
2. **A correct invoice.** Without one, there is no right to deduct. It must carry: the company name, legal form, enterprise number, address, a description of the goods/services, and the correct VAT rate. [astro]
   - **No valid invoice -> no VAT recovery**, even if the cost itself is deductible: a fuel-station till receipt, a parking-machine ticket, an NMBS train ticket. A **fuel card billed on a proper invoice** does give (partial) VAT recovery. [astro]
3. **The activity gives a right to deduction.** The purchase must serve taxed output activity; a VAT-**exempt** activity under art. 44 (doctors, schools, some others) gives **no** input-VAT deduction.

> Caps and exclusions still apply on top (e.g. [[restaurant-and-meals|restaurant VAT not deductible]], car/ICT capped). Confirm specifics with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be).
